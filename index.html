About

I'm a photographer and missionary currently based in Mombasa, Kenya, where the light is generous and the stories are endless. My work explores the intersection of place and spirit—the way landscapes hold memory, the way streets reveal character, the way architecture speaks of human aspiration and limitation.

After years of documenting the American landscape, my family and I moved to East Africa, trading familiar terrain for new ways of seeing. The transition has been both disorienting and revelatory, pushing me to reconsider everything I thought I knew about composition, narrative, and the ethics of representation.

My photography has been featured in various publications, and I continue to work on long-form photo essays that examine themes of migration, community, and the search for home. When I'm not behind the camera, I'm developing theological curriculum, running solo TTRPG campaigns, and trying to convince my kids that yes, we really do live here now.
